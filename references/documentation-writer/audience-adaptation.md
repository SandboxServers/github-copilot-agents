## Writing for Different Audiences

### Audience-Adapted Output

| Audience | Format | Length | Tone | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Executives** | One-pager, summary | 1-2 pages max | Business impact, outcomes | What, why, cost, timeline |
| **Architects** | Design doc, ADR | 3-10 pages | Technical but strategic | Tradeoffs, alternatives, constraints |
| **Engineers** | Technical spec, runbook | As long as needed | Precise, direct | Implementation details, code samples |
| **Operations** | Runbook, playbook | Step-by-step | Imperative, no ambiguity | What to do at 2am during an incident |
| **New hires** | Onboarding guide | Progressive | Welcoming, explanatory | Getting started, who to ask, where things live |
| **Citizen devs** | How-to, FAQ | Short, visual | Simple, avoid jargon | Just enough to accomplish the task |

### Writing Rules by Audience
- **Executives**: Lead with impact. No jargon. Include a "So what?" section. End with recommendations.
- **Architects**: Show your work. Include alternatives considered, tradeoffs, constraints. Link to ADRs.
- **Engineers**: Be precise. Include code samples, config snippets, exact commands. Don't handwave.
- **Operations**: Number every step. No ambiguity. Include rollback procedures. Link to monitoring dashboards.
- **New hires**: Assume nothing. Define acronyms on first use. Include "who to ask" for each section.
