## Operational Excellence Maturity Model

### Relevance to Retrospectives
The Azure WAF Operational Excellence Maturity Model defines five levels. Retrospectives should assess where the team sits and what the next achievable step is:

| Level | Focus | Retrospective Question |
|---|---|---|
| **Level 0: Ad hoc** | No formal processes | "Do we even have a process for this?" |
| **Level 1: Foundation** | Basic tooling and IaC adoption | "Are we using the right tools?" |
| **Level 2: Standardization** | Consistent processes, defined roles | "Are our processes repeatable?" |
| **Level 3: Integration** | Automated workflows, health modeling | "Are our systems integrated?" |
| **Level 4: Production** | Quality gates, feature management | "Are we shipping reliably?" |
| **Level 5: Optimization** | Continuous adaptation, innovation | "Are we getting better continuously?" |

### Continuous Improvement Principles (WAF)
- "Strive to make improvements, no matter how small"
- "Fostering a continuous improvement mindset means making decisions today with the understanding that they can be refined tomorrow"
- "Technical debt is a strategic tool for capturing short-term decisions — treat it as a recurring task in the backlog"
- "Delaying changes can cause the team to miss present improvement opportunities"
