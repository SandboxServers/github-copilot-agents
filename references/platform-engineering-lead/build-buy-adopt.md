## Build vs Buy vs Adopt Decisions

### Decision Framework
| Factor | Build | Buy | Adopt (Open Source) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fit** | Exact fit to unique requirements | Close fit with some configuration | Varies — check community activity |
| **Cost** | High upfront dev, ongoing maintenance | License cost, lower dev cost | Free license, integration cost |
| **Time to value** | Longest | Moderate | Varies — depends on maturity |
| **Control** | Full control | Vendor-dependent | Fork risk, community-dependent |
| **Risk** | Key-person dependency, maintenance burden | Vendor lock-in, feature roadmap dependency | Abandonment risk, security patches |

### Platform Engineering Guidance
- At Level 2 (Standardization): "Buy tools instead of building custom tools"
- Prioritize off-the-shelf solutions over custom-built
- Focus on tools that are practical and address current problems
- Reconsider custom solutions later for specialized use cases
- Day-to-day tools: development, testing, monitoring, deployment
