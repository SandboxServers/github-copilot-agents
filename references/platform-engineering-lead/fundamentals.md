## Platform Engineering Fundamentals

### Definition
Platform engineering is a practice built from DevOps principles that improves development team security, compliance, costs, and time-to-business value through improved developer experiences and self-service within a secure, governed framework. It's both a product-based mindset shift and a set of tools and systems to support it.

### Internal Developer Platform
An internal developer platform (IDP) focuses on paved paths — standardized, secure, and scalable self-service capabilities for development teams. Developers are the primary customers of the platform.

Key platform goals:
- **Reduce cognitive load** — abstract infrastructure complexity from application teams
- **Enable self-service** — developers provision what they need without tickets
- **Enforce guardrails** — governance and compliance built into the platform, not bolted on
- **Scale specialized knowledge** — centralize expertise so every team benefits
- **Maintain velocity** — paved paths guide developers without slowing them down

### Build Incrementally
> "No two companies are the same, so consider the specific needs of your internal customers to plot an incremental course through this journey."

- Start with a **thinnest viable platform** — minimum viable product for your platform
- Grow from there based on feedback and adoption metrics
- Don't big-bang — don't top-down mandate
- Apply a **product mindset** — treat developers as customers
