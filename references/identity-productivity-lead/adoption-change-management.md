## Adoption and Change Management

### The Adoption Problem

The most secure, well-governed M365 platform that nobody uses is a failure. Technology deployment without adoption strategy is just infrastructure.

### Champions Program

Champions are peer advocates embedded in each department:

- **Recruit**: Identify enthusiastic users who already help colleagues — formalize the role
- **Train**: Give champions early access to new features, dedicated training, direct channel to IT
- **Empower**: Champions run lunch-and-learns, answer peer questions, report adoption blockers
- **Recognize**: Visible recognition from leadership, champion community events, branded swag
- **Scale**: Target 1 champion per 50-100 users across all departments

### Training Strategy

| Approach | When to Use | Format |
|----------|------------|--------|
| **Self-service** | Ongoing skill building, new hire onboarding | Microsoft 365 learning pathways (customizable), short video tutorials, quick reference cards |
| **Instructor-led** | Major rollouts, complex scenarios, executive training | Live workshops (virtual or in-person), hands-on labs, role-based sessions |
| **Scenario-based** | Driving adoption of specific workflows | "How to run a project in Teams" not "How to use Planner" — teach outcomes, not features |
| **Just-in-time** | Feature changes, new capabilities | Pop-up tips, email digests, Teams channel announcements, champion-led demos |

### Change Management for Major Rollouts

**Communication plan** — answer five questions before any change:
1. What is changing?
2. Why is it changing?
3. When does it take effect?
4. Who is affected?
5. Where do I get help?

**Phased rollout pattern:**
1. **Pilot group** (2-4 weeks): IT staff and willing volunteers — validate configuration, identify issues
2. **Early adopters** (2-4 weeks): Champions and engaged departments — refine training, gather feedback
3. **Broad deployment**: All users — leverage champion network for peer support

**Support readiness**: Help desk trained and documented before users encounter changes. Create knowledge base articles, update support scripts, brief tier-1 support.

### Measuring Adoption

| Metric | Source | What It Tells You |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| **Active users per app** | M365 admin center usage reports | Which services are being used and by whom |
| **Feature adoption** | Usage reports (Teams channels, SharePoint sites, Power Apps) | Depth of adoption beyond basic login |
| **Help desk tickets** | ITSM system | Friction points — what's confusing or broken |
| **Champion feedback** | Champion community channel | Qualitative sentiment, blockers, feature requests |
| **User satisfaction** | Surveys (quarterly) | Overall platform perception and pain points |

### Resistance Management

- **"I already have a way that works"**: Show the value of the new approach in their workflow context — don't force migration, demonstrate benefit
- **"This is too complicated"**: Simplify the initial experience — hide advanced features, provide step-by-step guides
- **"IT keeps changing things"**: Improve communication cadence — change fatigue is real, space out rollouts
- **"I don't have time to learn"**: Provide 5-minute micro-training, embed learning in the flow of work
- **"My data isn't safe in the cloud"**: Address with specific security controls — show Conditional Access, encryption, compliance certifications
