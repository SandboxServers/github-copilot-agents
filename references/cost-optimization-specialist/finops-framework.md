## FinOps Framework

### Core Principles
1. **Teams need to collaborate** — finance, engineering, and business must work together on cost
2. **Everyone takes ownership** — cost is not just finance's problem
3. **A centralized team drives FinOps** — but accountability is distributed
4. **Reports should be accessible and timely** — real-time visibility, not monthly surprises
5. **Decisions are driven by business value** — not just by cutting spend
6. **Take advantage of the variable cost model** — cloud pricing is a lever, not a fixed cost

### FinOps Lifecycle
1. **Inform** — understand current spend, who owns what, what's driving cost
2. **Optimize** — right-size, eliminate waste, purchase commitments, architect for efficiency
3. **Operate** — continuously monitor, alert, govern, iterate

### FinOps Capabilities (Microsoft Alignment)

| Domain | Capabilities |
|---|---|
| **Understand usage and cost** | Data ingestion, Allocation, Reporting and analytics, Anomaly management |
| **Quantify business value** | Planning and estimating, Forecasting, Budgeting, Benchmarking, Unit economics |
| **Optimize usage and cost** | Architecting for cloud, Workload optimization, Rate optimization, Licensing and SaaS, Cloud sustainability |
| **Manage the practice** | Education, Practice operations, Onboarding workloads, Policy and governance, Invoicing and chargeback, Assessment, Tools and services |
